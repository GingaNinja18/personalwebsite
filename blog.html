10 Reasons Why Having a Personal Resume/Portfolio Website Helps with Job Searches and Getting Hired

    "On average, about 250 candidates apply for a corporate opening. Up to 6 of them will be interviewed, and only 1 will get the job. That’s a bummer. It seems like going that extra mile to stand out from the crowd does make sense. And creating a resume website might just be the way to go." -Inc
    In the digital age, job hunting has evolved. Traditional resumes and cover letters are still important, but having a personal resume/portfolio website can give you an edge in the competitive job market. Here are 10 reasons why:
  1. Showcase Your Work: A personal website allows you to display your work in a way that a paper resume can’t. Whether you’re a designer, writer, or engineer, you can use your site to showcase your projects, case studies, or code snippets.
  2. Control Your Online Presence: With a personal website, you control the narrative. You decide what information potential employers see when they Google your name.
  3. Stand Out from the Crowd: A well-designed, professional website can help you stand out from other candidates. It shows you’ve taken the extra step to present yourself and your work in the best possible light.
  4. Demonstrate Your Skills: A website can be a testament to your skills. For instance, if you’re applying for a web development position, what better way to prove your skills than with a site you built yourself?
  5. Expand on Your Resume: A resume is typically a one-page document. A personal website gives you the space to expand on your experience, skills, and education.
  6. 24/7 Accessibility: Your website is like a digital business card that’s accessible 24/7. Potential employers can learn about you and your work anytime, anywhere.
  7. Networking Opportunities: Your website can help you connect with others in your field. By including a blog or news section, you can share your thoughts on industry trends, which can lead to discussions and networking opportunities.
  8. Flexibility: With a personal website, you can quickly and easily update your portfolio and resume as you gain new skills or complete new projects.
  9. Professionalism: Having a personal website sends a message that you’re serious about your career and that you’ve put in the effort to present yourself professionally online.
  10. Personal Branding: A personal website allows you to build your personal brand. You can express your personality, values, and career goals, helping potential employers see if you’re a good fit for their company culture.
    11. In conclusion, a personal resume/portfolio website is a powerful tool in your job search arsenal. It allows you to showcase your work, control your online presence, and stand out from the crowd. So why wait? Start building your personal website today!
